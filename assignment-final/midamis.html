Midamis

Strange Alien World

The drawings down below exhibit the artist's eye for detail and environment design along with his eye for creature design that he has worked to refine over the years. This world shows off concepts of biology that differ from Earth, yet are simultaneouly familiar to what we know of biology found in our world. Pandora from Avatar was a major inspiration to the animals shown below.

The World

This gas giant roughly the size of Saturn takes 8 Earth hours to complete a full rotation on its axis! The year for the gas giant
takes approximately 500 Earth days! This is one of the few gas giants known to science to sit comfortably in the habitable zone! the
zone in which water can be sustained without freezing or vaporizing! The planets largest moon, Midamis, supports an enormous array of unusual lifeforms! The moon's gravity is incredibly low! Its so miniscule that almost 90 percent of the planets animals have evolved to swim in the air, hover, or float, using specialized
sacs of hydrogen located within their bodies and fin like organs, wings, or even jet like structures to propel themselves! The planet is also dominated by unusual photosynthesizing
animals called "Photocnidarians"!so-called because they all resemble hybrids between plant-life and cydarians on Earth such as jellyfish and anemones! They outnumber the plant life on
this world and very few are capable of locomotion! The planet is almost all forest, dotted by large clearings of grassland and ocean!
So obviously similar to Pandora, except I wanted to also think about something truly alien unique to this world while also making sense to biology, thus I chose animals with a unique adaptation to the very low gravity and animals that have characteristics of both plant and animal! The moon is named after Midas, the story of the king, named Midas, who turns everything he touces into gold, referring to the planet being a rare jewel in the cosmos, a world that can support life!

Family Bond

A fully matured Staghorn with its infant! The mother must be cautious at all times, as predators can come and attack at any moment!
This Midamis inhabitant is roughly the size of a school bus and uses its enormous antlers to threaten or attack territorial intruders
or deadly predators!
So it was probably a mistake to set my first drawing in the moon's jungles but was pleasantly surpised with how it turned out, applying a plethora of different creatures, plants, etc scattered all over the place. One can find several interesting things in this one drawing alone!

A Well-Earned Feast

The deadliest low-ground predator on Midamis, the Whip-Tailed Gnasher, feeds on a common grassland grazer. This predator has two rows of razor-sharp
teeth that can go through bone with ease and a tail it can whip at other Gnashers for dominance! The darkness of the forest looms deep, making it impossible
to see the activity crawling inside! But the life in it is quite audible!
This apex predator was very fun to design, thinking about aerodynamic predators on Earth, such as a Tiger, and applying it into its look! Finally, giving it interesting body parts, like its tail, to finalize the creature!

An Ocean in the Sky

One of Midamis's largest floating animals, the appropriately name Sky Whale is only 5 feet shorter then the Blue Whale on Earth! This incredible animal swims
in the clouds, feeding on the microscopic fliers, who in turn feed on large Photocnidarians that also drift in the air,or the giant ones that tower over the clouds
from the forest canopies! They use there large feathered appendages jutting out of there face to brush any "micro-fliers" into there gaping maw! This particular specimen
has a group of animals called Dermiskates picking off any parasitic micro-fliers that might be attached to its rubbery skin! The Sky Whale has one of the largest internal hydrogen
sacs of any Midamin animal!
I have taken a lot of Earth ocean animals and adapted them to flying, floating creatures for Midamis, including this creature, that traps tiny, flying creatures for consumption!
